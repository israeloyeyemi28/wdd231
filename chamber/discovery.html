History Of Badagry

Founded in 1425 A.D by the indigines of the land and was originally called Agbedegreme, named after a farmer called Agbedeh. Later it was corrupted to Agbedagari by the Yoruba settlersand then badagry by the European slave traders

Badagry became a major slave port in the 18th century and was involved in the exportation of slaves to the Americas. The British established a trading post in Badagry in the 1820's.

In 1842, it became the site for the first European Mission (Methodist) in Nigeria. Badagry was annexed by the United Kingdom and incorporated into the Lagos colony in 1863.

The town became a part in Nigeria in 1901, the name Badagry is derived from the occupation of the people which included fishing, farming and making salt
